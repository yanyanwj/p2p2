When we talk to the online customer service, most of them will respond quickly. Some of them are workers on duty, others are fictional human-set bots. CHATBOT CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

According to a taxonomy of models, chatbot is basically divided into two models. One is called Retrieval-based Model and the other is called Generative Model.And the domain that chatbot covers is divided into open domain and closed domain.

retrieval-based models

Usually retrieve-based models first are needed to build a knowledge graph.
When the knowledge graph is large enough, it may cause delay in retrieval.
So how to retrieve is the first problem that such chatbot solves.
These systems don’t generate any new text,
they just pick a response from a fixed set.
There is a way to make them smarter,
which is to use text classification to add a intent for chatbot.

generative models

The generative models can generate text which is not set by humans.
This chatbot uses A Neural Conversational Model for two conversations.
One is film dialoge, which is used for chat and conversation.
The other is IT support dialogue,
which can both solve problems and greet users.
The generative model is data-drive,
neither an artificial input nor a fixed set.

open domain

In an open domain setting the user can take the conversation anywhere.
There isn’t necessarily have a well-defined goal or intention.
The infinite number of topics and the fact that a amount of world knowledge
is required to create reasonable responses makes this a hard problem.

closed domain

In a closed domain setting the space of possible inputs and outputs is
somewhat limited because the system is trying to achieve a very specific goal.
Technical Customer Support or Shopping Assistants are examples of
closed domain problems.

02

Challenges

Incorporating Context

To produce sensible responses systems may need to incorporate both linguistic context and physical context. In long dialogs people keep track of what has been said and what information has been exchanged.One may also need to use other data such as date/time and location

Coherent Personality

When generating responses the agent should produce consistent answers to semantically identical inputs. For example, you want to get the same reply to “How old are you?” and “What is your age?”.

Evaluation of Models

The ideal way to evaluate a conversational agent is to measure whether or not it is fulfilling its task.But metrics correlate with human judgment are complicated.

Intention and Diversity

Humans typically produce responses that are specific to the input and carry an intention.
